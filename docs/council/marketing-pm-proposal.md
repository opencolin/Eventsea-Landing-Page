# Marketing PM Proposal — 4-Week Sprint to Qualified Inbound

**Author:** Marketing PM (Founding Council)
**Date:** 2026-06-11
**Decision asked:** Approve a single 4-week marketing plan to drive qualified inbound to eventsea.ai and warm up Nebius, vCluster, Tavily, LanceDB.

## 1. Strategic frame

One unfair asset nobody else in events space has: a working radar prototype (`event-radar-mvp.phantastic.ai`) and a wedge artifact (the AI calendar audit) that produces a defensible, shareable, screenshot-worthy output. Everything else (Twitter, LinkedIn, GitHub, blog, SEO) is empty.

**We are not doing content marketing this sprint. We are doing proof marketing**: publish audits of calendars that target customers already care about; make those audits the inbound funnel.

Positioning anchor: "Stop guessing which conferences are worth it." The audit *is* the proof of the positioning.

## 2. The ONE launch channel: Show HN

**Pick:** Show HN. **Why:** The 4 named targets + AI infra field marketing audience live on HN. A single front-page Show HN delivers (a) qualified inbound, (b) a credibility receipt linkable from every future cold email, (c) social proof for Twitter/LinkedIn amplification.

ProductHunt indexes wrong audience. Paid ads premature without conversion data. Twitter/LinkedIn become *amplifiers*, not the launch.

The Show HN artifact is NOT "we built a landing page." It is **"I audited 200 AI conferences and here's what the data says."** Audit corpus = news hook; product = byline.

## 4. Week-by-week

**Week 1 — Build the corpus.** A1 + A2 drafted. A9 (social accounts seeded). Pick HN posting day. Identify outbound targets and audit owners.

**Week 2 — Build the proof stack.** Publish A3 (YC audit) + A4 (AI Engineer audit) with no fanfare — they need to be live and indexable before HN. Deliver A5 audits 1–2 (Nebius, vCluster).

**Week 3 — Launch.** Deliver A5 audits 3–4 (Tavily, LanceDB). Monday: outbound touch 1 to all 4 named targets with private audit attached. **Tuesday 9am PT: Show HN.** Twitter (A7) at +2h. LinkedIn (A8) at +24h. Council monitors comments; founder responds to every top-level within 30min for first 6 hours.

**Week 4 — Convert.** A11 published. Outbound touches 2 + 3. Book demo calls. Second-wave Twitter thread with HN reactions. Update hero to surface Show HN result.

## 5. What I would NOT do

- **No paid ads.** Zero conversion baseline; we'd pay to learn what HN teaches us free.
- **No ProductHunt.** Wrong audience.
- **No SEO content factory.** 4 weeks too short for SEO. Write 4 hand-picked link-bait posts instead.
- **No webinar / podcast tour.** Slow to schedule, hard to attribute.
- **No relaunch of the landing page IA.** Audience-page work is out of scope; if we redo IA before Show HN we slip the launch.
- **No public audit of a target customer.** Targets get *private* audits. Public audits are YC, AI Engineer Summit, neutral calendars.
- **No beta gate.** Form already accepts URL → email. Waitlists kill HN-day conversion.

## 6. Risks + mitigations

- **Show HN flops.** Council prewarms with 5 substantive comments first hour. If it dies, repurpose A2 as paid LinkedIn promotion.
- **Named target reacts negatively.** Frame outbound as "we built this for you, your data stays private."
- **Audit quality embarrasses.** Council reviews every public audit pre-publish.

## 7. Success metric

**Primary (binary):** 2 of 4 named targets (Nebius, vCluster, Tavily, LanceDB) take a 30-minute discovery call by end of Week 4.

**Secondary:**
- Show HN: top 10 ≥2 hours (≥150 points, ≥80 comments)
- 250 unique calendar-audit submissions during sprint
- 40 qualified inbound leads (work email, real company, AI infra/devtools/field marketing title)
- Founder Twitter: +500 followers, ≥3 follows from named-target employees

## Blockers asked of council

- (a) Founder sign-off on running radar over 4 named targets without permission
- (b) One content lead allocated full 4 weeks
- (c) eventsea Twitter/LinkedIn/GitHub spun up by end of Week 1
